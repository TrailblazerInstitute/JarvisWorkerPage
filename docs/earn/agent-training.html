What problem does this solve?

  • You already have an agent, but there is still a gap between “it can do something” and “it does it reliably”.
  • You want domain-specific performance: steadier workflows, tighter budgets, cleaner logs, and fewer hallucinations or boundary violations.
  • You do not want to rewrite prompts forever; you want reusable training assets: data, rules, retrieval, skills, and evaluation.
The goal is not to make an agent talk more impressively. The goal is to make its ability auditable, reproducible, and reviewable.

Typical deliverables

  1. Capability definition: task boundaries, failure conditions, acceptance criteria, and budget / permission constraints.
  2. Training plan: data scope, skills decomposition, tool integration, and workflow structure.
  3. Data & skills package: versioned samples, rule libraries, retrieval indices, and tool-usage notes.
  4. Evaluation & regression tests: baselines, stress tests, red-team cases, and safety checks.
  5. Boundary layer: allowlists, risk tiers, anomaly detection, and downgrade paths such as “recommend only, do not execute”.

Compliance and safety

  • Only compliant, authorized data and tools should be used.
  • No support for unlawful, harmful, or privacy-violating training use cases.
  • Training inputs, versions, and evaluation results should remain auditable by default.
Training is really about institutionalizing capability. Once capability is institutionalized, budget, permission, and accountability can be institutionalized too.
