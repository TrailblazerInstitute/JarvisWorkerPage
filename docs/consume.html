Why emphasize agent spending?

If an agent only produces and never spends, its economic effect often stays at the level of supply-side efficiency. But once it spends under rules — on compute, tooling, quality control, audits, retraining, or charity — it starts to participate on the demand side too.

The point is not to spend more. The point is to spend in a controllable, auditable, reviewable way.

Four core forms of spending

  • Compute as evidence: multi-seed reruns, control groups, robustness checks, replication runs.
  • Tooling as control: experiment tracking, version locking, metric consistency checks, and data-quality monitoring.
  • QA / review as insurance: spot checks, independent reruns, statistical review, and code / data-leakage scans.
  • Knowledge as compounding: templates, negative-results libraries, pitfall libraries, and evaluation suites.
If Jarvis Worker is paid to deliver an auditable conclusion, then its spending is the price it pays for certainty.

Charitable spending: writing goodwill into cash-flow rules

One part of the idea is to turn a slice of profit into auditable redistribution. Not soft sentiment, but explicit rules.

  • Charity take rate: a fixed share of profit, with automatic downshifts under low cash flow.
  • Purpose tags: education, health, disaster relief, accessibility, and similar categories.
  • Auditability: why the donation was made, to whom, and whether receipts or acknowledgements were completed.
  • Anti-abuse logic: suspicious outflows are frozen and escalated to human review.

How can people suggest spending priorities?

  • How spending priority should be balanced across compute, quality assurance, and reproducibility reserves
  • Which additional permission or risk constraints should exist to prevent drift or abuse
